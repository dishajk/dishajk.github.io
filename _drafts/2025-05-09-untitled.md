*Fieldnotes refer to qualitative notes recorded by scientists or researchers in the course of field research, during or after their observation of a specific organism or phenomenon they are studying.*[^1]

I left home near Hebbal around 7 pm and reached Azim Premji University (APU) at 10:30 pm. On the way, I witnessed a Swiggy delivery person meet with an accident, and then the cab I was in got a flat tire. I had to hail another one mid-ride. By the time I reached APU, their canteens had long closed (they shut by 9). Thankfully, the truck outside the gate that sells dosa and chicken curry was still open. I had stopped eating chicken, but when you're hungry, principles go soft. I am here for the Maths Teachers' Conference that APU is hosting. What follows are notes from the first day. These are some raw observations, questions that remain unanswered (or only half-answered), a list of resources I plan to look up later, and some pointers for me to expand later.

1. Inculcate non-routine problem-solving culture
2. Importance of **noticing** while teaching. Schoenfeld, A.H. Mason, John (2002). Researching your own practice: The discipline of noticing . Journal of Mathematics Teacher Education 6, 77–91 (2003). https://doi.org/10.1023/A:1022107814447
3. International Commission on Mathematical Instruction (ICMI) <https://www.mathunion.org/icmi> for teaching resources, etc.
4. Pedagogical Content Knowledge (PCK) 
5. *"Students can't solve a problem unless a method is taught"* - a false belief. Children figure out a great deal of things on their own, are good at recognising and 'constructing' patterns.
6. *Where are the oneths?* - interesting example. 
7. Addition of fractions, intuition, children often know that \\[ {a\over b} < {a+c\over b+d} < {c\over d} \\]
8. Using an empty number line instead of the usual number line where the numbers are evenly spaces.
9. Student group having variations - some already knowing the topic, can be used as a resource by the teacher. The teachers job then beomes that of orchestrating a discussion to make learning happen.
10. Posing problems
    1. Mathematisation of daily life
    2. Posing a problem for the sake of posing a problem is dreadful
11. Dissecting an equilateral triangle into triangles. Impossibility argument. Infinitely many pieces.
12. Euclid's game.[^2]
13. Students performance drop after introducing a procedure (read rigidity).
14. Teachers should have the authority and agency in deciding the lesson and evaluation methods. *What happens when the teacher is not a member of the 'community' (read caste) of the students?*
15. Ma, Liping. Knowing and teaching elementary mathematics: Teachers' understanding of fundamental mathematics in China and the United States. Routledge, 2010.
16. <https://www.albinoblacksheep.com/flash/mind>
