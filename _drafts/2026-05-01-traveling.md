I travelled a lot while in college. The magazines we read while in school: Reader's Digest, National Geographic, TerraWorld (I had a 3 Years subscription), India Today, Sanctuary Asia, Tinkle, The Week, all of these had an article or a story or two about a place outside Bangalore. Some of them would even have iterinaries for what to do in some of these places. I read them, collected some very interesting ones. The plan was to check them off one by one.

College was the first time I was away from home for long enough for me to plan and execute this plan. Of course I wouldn't tell at home that I am planning to go places for no reason but to just see the place. Who does that sort of thing? You travel with a purpose. Seeing a place isn't a valid enough reason unless the place had, say, a wonder of the world or something. I had had already seen Taj Mahal on one of the trips while in school.

If I don't tell at home that I am traveling, it also means that I am traveling with no money from home, with only whatever little savings I had. Money was tight. But then I had a lot of energy and a lot of time. I can take the train and public bus. The cheapest of them. Trains weren't as expensive as they are today. Forget about AC buses, if there was no seat in the bus, you would sit on the bus, like everyone else that didn't get a sit inside the bus. All of this also meant that I was traveling alone. There weren't many that considered it smart to travel like that, especially as a woman in India. People around me were saner, they would pause and think about the safety. Possibility of getting sexually assaulted vs. being in the mist covered mountains off Pune. I ended up going to a lot of these places alone. I bought myself a youth hostel lifetime membership (I wouldn't recommend it anymore, they no longer serve the purpose they originally were created for. You should simply book a hotel, it'll cost you the same.).

Traveling also meant that I had postcards from a lot of places. These postcards grew. It is when redecorating my living room recently that I realised that I don't have any new postcards. This made me realise that I have not been traveling. This made me realise that I no longer have the time to travel because of having a job. If I quit my job, I will no longer have the money to pay rent for the room to put up the postcards. I decided to _make_ time to travel, whatever that means, make use of those "long weekends" and such.

But then in three days, you can't go that far. Public transport takes too much time, unless its a premium train or a flight. This restricts the kinds of places one can go to. No offbeat places. You go where everyone goes. This means no risky situtations. You see, when considering traveling alone to a beach or a mountain that nobody goes to, the probability of getting attacked by a man is very low. You only have to worry about natural disasters and wild animals. I have survived floods and cyclones. Wild animals weren't as interested in me as much as I was in them. 

Last week a friend suggested that I extend my trip to Chennai, go someplace near Chennai and stop complaining about not going anywhere. I was going to Chennai for work, the kind that is mentally exhausting. I can extend myself physically, I can't mentally. I am a disaster on legs when on auto-pilot. I decided that I will find a quiet place to read. I told this to people around me. People suggested Kodaikanal. I decided to go to Kodaikanal and read.

And read I did. I will write about what I read.
